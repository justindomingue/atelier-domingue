# Drafting the Back — Historical Tailoring Masterclasses

Source: https://masterclasses.historical-tailoring.com/lesson/drafting-the-back-6/

Start with the legs, work up towards the waist. The back is drafted relative to the front panel.

## Back Leg Widths

1. Extend the hem and knee lines by **3/4"**. This accounts for the 3/8" we took off the front widths and gives an additional 3/8" ease.
2. Extend the crotch line from point 6. **6–11** is half the seat measurement divided by 8 (= seat/16).
3. Draw construction lines from 11 through the new knee line (call it **12**) to the hem.
4. Measure from 12 to 6 on the front pattern piece.
5. The back leg from **12 to 11** = **6 to 12 minus 1/4"**. (Denim adjustment — no easing possible, accounts for natural stretch.)
6. Draw the curve of the back inseam from **11 to 12**.

## The Seat Angle

7. Mark **3/8"** from point **2** on the side seam going towards point 4. (New point above 2.)
8. Extend the hip line from 4 past **8** by **1"**. (1" ease per side = 2" total for full hip.)
9. Draw a line from the new point above 2 through the new point at 8.
10. From point 8, draw a line **at right angles** to the line you just drew, up towards and beyond the waist. This is the **seat angle**.
11. Draw a shallow curve from the new point at 8 through 11. At 8, blend with the seat angle line. At 11, meet at close to 90 degrees.

## The Waist Seam

12. Draw a line from **point 1** (the original, not the curved one) towards the seat angle line, meeting at **90 degrees**. Extend past the seat angle line a couple of inches.
13. Measure the front waist seam from the 3/8 mark to the 5/8 mark.
14. Back waist = front waist + 3/4" to half waist measurement. Mark on the back waist line. (The 3/4" makes room for two darts in the yoke.)
15. Redraw the seat seam from the new back waist point, starting at **90 degrees to the waist**, and gently curving into point 8 and the crotch curve.

## Double Checking

- **Waist**: Measure waist lines, subtract 3/4". Double it — should match waist measurement.
- **Hips**: Front 4→8 + back 4→seat seam (at 90° to seat seam). Should have a couple inches ease.
- **Fly/Seat seams**: Check lengths.
- **Lengths**: Side lengths and inseam correct? (Side length measurement should not include waistband.) Back curved inseam 1/4" shorter than front?
