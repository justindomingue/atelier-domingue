# Drafting the Yoke — 1873

Source: https://masterclasses.historical-tailoring.com/lesson/drafting-the-yoke-1873/

The yoke is drafted after the back panel is complete, before cutting out and
adding seam allowances. It separates the upper back panel (waist to yoke line)
from the lower back panel.

## Prerequisites

- Completed front panel draft (points 0–10, 1\*, 7\*, 0', 3')
- Completed back panel draft (points 11, 12, back\_waist, 8\_new, etc.)

## Point 13

The original lesson references **point 13**, which is not numbered in our back
panel code. Based on context, point 13 corresponds to our `back_waist` point —
the endpoint of the back waist line on the seat-seam side.

## Steps

### 1. Mark the yoke depth on both sides

- On the **side seam** (outseam): measure **1 1/2"** from point **1** toward
  the hem and mark. This is the yoke point on the side seam side.
- On the **seat seam** side: measure **2 3/4"** from point **13**
  (`back_waist`) along the seat seam toward 8\_new and mark. This is the yoke
  point on the seat seam side.

> These widths are a starting point. If you have very high-rise jeans, you may
> need to deepen the yoke. Best determined after the first fitting.

### 2. Draw the yoke line

Connect the two yoke points with a straight line. This divides the back panel
into the yoke (above) and the lower back (below).

### 3. Mark the dart position

Divide the **back waist line** (from point 1 to `back_waist`) into **3 equal
sections**, marking the two division points.

The dart will be placed at the division point **closest to the side seam**
(i.e. closest to point 1).

### 4. Draw the dart guideline

From the dart position on the waist, draw a line **square (perpendicular) to
the waist** down to the yoke line.

### 5. Draw the dart

Draw a **3/4" wide dart** centered on the guideline:
- **3/8"** on each side of the guideline
- The dart runs from the waist line down to the yoke line

In the 1873 jeans, the dart functions as a simple **pleat** rather than a
stitched dart. The waist size was adjusted by varying the pleat width.

### 6. Trace and cut

- Trace out the yoke piece (the area between the waist line and the yoke line)
  including the dart.
- Add seam allowances to all edges.
- Cut out the yoke.

## Notes

- The 1873 yoke is relatively simple/straight compared to modern curved yokes,
  but both yield the same functional result.
- The dart/pleat provides shaping to accommodate the curve from waist to hip.
- The 3/4" dart accounts for the **3/4" ease** added to the back waist width
  in the back panel drafting step (step 14 in back instructions).
