# Muslin Toile Construction Guide — Selvedge Denim Jeans

Reference instructions for sewing the muslin toile, organized by construction stage. Use alongside your drafted pattern pieces.

---

## 1. Cutting the Muslin Toile

**Pieces required:** 2 Front Legs, 2 Back Legs, 2 Yokes, 1 Waistband, 1 Fly

### Layout

- Lay all pattern pieces on muslin with side seams along the selvedge or aligned with the grain line.
- The yoke should ideally match the grain direction of the legs. (Rotating to save fabric is acceptable for a fitting toile only.)
- Place the waistband and one-piece fly on the fold.

### Marking

- Mark along both sides of the pattern for the **hem, knee, crotch, and hip lines**. These reference lines make fitting easier.
- Flip the pattern back at the side seam edge to make marks, then connect with a ruler to draw lines across each piece.
- Transfer all marks to the corresponding piece underneath.

### Cutting

- Carefully cut out each piece once all marks and lines are transferred.

---

## 2. Sew the Back

### Yoke Preparation

- Mark 3/8" along the yoke seam of the back leg piece, then **trim off this 3/8"** from the back piece. (This extra is used in the modern jeans construction only.)

### Attaching the Yoke

- Pin the yoke to the back leg, **right sides together**. The **taller end of the yoke goes on the seat seam**, not the side seam.
- Pin the other side of the yoke as well.
- For 1873 historical jeans: there should be a bit of ease in the back leg at the yoke seam. For the modern pair, lengths should match exactly.
- Pin the center of the yoke seam, distributing fullness equally on both sides, then subdivide each half with additional pins.
- Sew using a **3/8" seam allowance**, keeping the back leg on the bottom so the presser foot helps distribute fullness.

### Pressing

- Open the yoke and **press the seam towards the yoke**. Keep the iron on the seam itself — avoid pressing out the fullness.
- Top stitch the yoke seam about **1/16" from the seam** through all layers.

### 1873 Jeans Only — Dart/Pleat

- Use the straight-cut yoke. After attaching, mark the top of the dart.
- Fold the two marks together forming a pleat, with the right side of the pleat pointing towards the back of the yoke (the tall side).
- Stitch across the top of the yoke **1/4" from the edge** to hold the pleat in place.

### Seat Seam

- Place the two back legs **right sides together**. Pin the seat seam, aligning the yoke seams and the hip and crotch lines.
- Sew using a **5/8" seam allowance**.
- Gently **stretch the seat seam at the curve** (in the seam allowance only) using the iron, until the curve can form a straight line.
- Press the seat seam **open**.
- **Check:** The hip line should be straight across. If not, your seat angle or sewing may be off.

---

## 3. The Left Fly

### Preparing the Fly Piece

- Press the long edges of the fly piece over by about **1/2"**.
- Press the fly in half lengthwise, aligning the folded edges.
- Sew the fly closed along the length using about **1/8" seam allowance**.

### Preparing the Left Leg

- Lay both front legs right side up. Work on the left side first.
- On the **wrong side**, fold back the fly extension by **3/8"** and press.
- Fold and press the entire front fly edge (waist to crotch) by another **3/8"**.
- Gently stretch the seam allowance with your fingers in the curved area. Use steam, especially in the curve, to set everything.
- On the right side, **mark the bottom of the fly extension** (feel for the difference in fabric thickness).
- Sew along the folded edge using a **3/8" seam allowance**, curving the seam near the bottom of the fly extension into the marked point.

### Installing the Fly on the Left Leg

- Lay the left front **wrong side up**. Place the fly about **1/8" behind the folded edge** on the fly seam, extending about **1" beyond the waist**.
- Pin the fly about an inch below the mark denoting the bottom of the fly extension.
- Turn the assembly over and realign as needed.
- Top stitch from the waist through all layers, keeping about **1/4" from the edge** of the fly underneath.
- Sew straight along the fly edge, then **curve towards the trouser edge** to meet the previous stitching at the mark.
- Turn to the wrong side and **press the unsewn half of the fly up over itself**.

---

## 4. The Right Fly

### Preparing the Right Leg

- On the right leg, mark the fly extension **3/8" from the edge**, then **cut off the fly extension**.

### Aligning and Sewing

- Lay the left leg assembly **right side down**. Fold the fly up towards itself.
- Lay the right leg **wrong side down on top**, aligning the fly area and waist seams between both legs.
- Mark the top of the fly just above the right leg waist seam (as a realignment reference).
- Pick up the right leg and free end of the fly. The left leg can be moved aside.
- At the machine, align the right leg with the chalk mark, set back **1/8" from the edge of the fly**.
- Sew a **3/8" seam** through the right leg and fly, from waist down to the bottom of the fly (where the extension used to end).
- Open the right fly — the narrow seam should fold nicely on top of the right leg. Press well.
- Sew a line **3/8" from the original stitching** along the edge of the fly, ending just at the bottom before it gets bulky.
- Fold the left fly half in place — the fly is now complete.

### Front Crotch Seam

- On the right leg curve, part of the seam has been turned back from sewing. Turn the rest back by the same amount.
- **Stretch the seam with your fingers** to give the fabric room to fold, then press over.
- Note how the left and right crotch seams naturally want to overlap. Maintain that spacing as you gradually overlap the layers moving toward the bottom of the curve.
- **Baste the seam closed** from the bottom edge towards the fly using a diagonal basting stitch, maintaining even spacing.
- Machine stitch from the bottom of the curve towards the fly, about **1/8" from the seam edge**.
- Stitch a second row ideally **3/8" from the first line**, catching all layers.
- **Trim the tops of the fly flush with the waist** (cut both halves together for consistency).

---

## 5. Join the Legs

### Inseam

- Place back legs on the table, right side up. Mark a **3/8" line** along the entire inseam on both legs.
- At the crotch seam, taper any point inward by up to **1/4"** if necessary (use a hip curve to smooth it out).
- Place front legs on top, **right sides together**. Align the front crotch seam with the back seat seam. The front leg edge should align with the 3/8" line.
- Pin at the knee line, hem, and in between as needed. If there's a length difference, gently stretch the back inseam to match.
- Sew the inseam **from one hem, through the crotch, to the other hem** using a **3/4" seam allowance** (or 3/8" from the upper layer edge).
- Press the inseam **open**.

### Side Seams

- Pin the top, hem, knee, and crotch marks together, then fill in additional pins.
- Sew using a **3/4" seam allowance** (for both pieces).
- Press the side seam, then turn the toile **right side out**.

---

## 6. Waistband

### Attaching the Waistband

- Find the left fly. Place the waistband on top, **right sides together**, with a few inches extending beyond the fly. Align the top edge with the waist.
- Sew from the left fly around the waist to the right fly, **2–3 inches at a time**, aligning as you go. Use a **3/8" seam allowance**.
- **Verify that the fly lengths match** on both sides before continuing.

### Pressing and Folding

- Press the waistband and seams **up towards the top** of the waistband.
- At each waistband end, square a line across from the edge of the fly (keep it square to the waistband, not angled).
- Press excess under (trim first if more than 1–2 inches).
- Measuring from the bottom of the waistband seam, mark a line **1 1/2" from the seam** — this is the top of the waistband.
- Fold and press the waistband under along this line. Fold in and press the ends.

### Top Stitching

- Starting from the left waistband, top stitch about **1/8" from the bottom of the waistband seam** through all layers.
- Ensure the inside fabric overlap gets caught in this seam. Smooth everything out every couple of inches.
- Adjust the ends so they're folded correctly before sewing through them.
- Finish top stitching at the edge of the right fly.

---

## 7. Hems

- Fold the hems along the marked hem line at the bottom of the legs.
- Turn and press the hem upward.

---

## Seam Allowance Quick Reference

| Seam | Allowance |
|------|-----------|
| Yoke to back leg | 3/8" |
| Yoke top stitch | 1/16" from seam |
| Seat seam | 5/8" |
| Fly piece closed | 1/8" |
| Fly extension fold-back | 3/8" |
| Fly top stitch | 1/4" from fly edge |
| Right fly attachment | 3/8" |
| Front crotch — first row | 1/8" from edge |
| Front crotch — second row | 3/8" from first row |
| Inseam | 3/4" |
| Side seams | 3/4" |
| Waistband | 3/8" |
| Waistband top stitch | 1/8" from seam |
| Waistband height | 1 1/2" from seam |
