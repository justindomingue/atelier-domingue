# Finishing the Jeans — Construction Guide

---

## 1. The Inseam

### Preparation
- Trim back and front crotch for a smooth transition (use a hip curve, ~3/16"). Overcast any newly cut areas.
- Mark a **3/8" seam allowance** on the back inseam, from hem through crotch to the other hem.
- Lay front legs on back legs, **right sides together**. Align front inseam edge with the marked line (just barely covering it).
- Pin at knee, hem, and crotch, then fill in. Gently stretch the back leg if the front has excess.
- Sew from hem through crotch to hem using a **3/4" seam allowance** (3/4" from back edge, 3/8" from front).

### Finishing — Modern (Flat-Felled)
- Press back leg seam allowance over the front inseam by ~3/8" (just before the stitch line).
- Open legs right side up. Top stitch about **3/8" from the seam** through all layers, hem to hem through crotch.
- Optional: add a second row **1/16"–1/8"** from the seam.
- Use a hammer to flatten layers at the crotch, and a seam jumper when sewing.

### Finishing — 1873 Method
- Press the seam allowance toward the **front leg** (larger allowance covering smaller).
- Top stitch from hem through crotch to hem, about **1/8" from the seam** through all layers.
- Check the underside frequently for shifting.

---

## 2. The Side Seam

### Sewing
- Open each leg to verify you're sewing the correct seams together.
- Fold each leg in half, right sides together, matching side seams at waist and hem.
- Sew from waist to hem using a **3/4" seam allowance**, keeping hip and knee marks aligned to avoid introducing a twist.

### Pocket Covering (Optional)
- Cut a rectangular piece of light/medium muslin, a couple inches longer than the pocket at the side seam, about **2 1/2" wide**.
- Press ~1/2" at one narrow end; press ~3/8" along one long edge.
- Lay on the pocket just inside the side seam's seam allowance, folded end meeting the curved seam. Keep the fold ~1/8" from the side seam.
- Sew along the folded edge to the waist seam. Press toward the selvedge, wrap around to the back side.
- Turn under the remaining raw edge (~1/8" from the sewn seam) and sew closed ~1/8" from the fold.

### Pressing & Top Stitching
- Press the side seam **open** from hem to a couple inches below the pocket.
- At the pocket area, press pocket and covering toward the back. Press front side seam at a diagonal so it opens fully.
- Turn right side out and press again.
- Mark the bottom of the pocket covering on each leg (ensure identical placement).
- Top stitch from waist through all pocket/seam layers to the mark, about **1/8" from the seam** toward the back leg.
- Trim excess from fly tops and pocket coverings flush with the waist.

---

## 3. Belt Loops I — Modern (Preparation)

### Positioning
- Use front pattern to find center line at the waist (where waistband meets pocket opening). Offset 1/2" to avoid the rivet.
- Mark another point ~1/2" past the side seam toward the back.
- On the back, evenly space 2–3 more loops; keep all loops evenly spaced.

### Cutting & Preparing
- Mark a length of denim (ideally on the selvedge), **4" minimum per loop**, about **1 1/2" wide** for a 1/2" finished loop.
- Press the raw edge under just under 1/2". Press the selvedge edge over to get the final 1/2" width.
- Top stitch along each long edge, about **1/16" from the edge**, catching the selvedge underneath.
- Cut into 4" lengths.

### Initial Attachment
- Place each loop **right sides together** on the waist mark, at right angles to the waist seam.
- Bar tack across about **1/4" from the waist** (will be hidden under waistband).

---

## 4. The Waistband

### Sewing
- Align the waistband to the left fly along the waist, **right sides together**, with ~2" extending beyond the fly edge.
- Sew from the left fly edge using a **3/8" seam allowance**, flipping back occasionally to check alignment. Use a seam gauge.
- Continue through to the right fly (another ~2" extra waistband).
- Ensure any pleats (1873) or belt loops are correctly positioned before sewing over them.

### Pressing & Folding
- Press waistband and seams up toward the top.
- Mark the fold line **1 1/2" from the waist seam**. Press in half along this line, keeping marks slightly to the inside.
- On the wrong side, mark lines **square with the waistband** at each fly edge. Press the ends in.
- Mark another two lines about **2" square from the fly edge** (room for buttonhole/button). Trim excess.
- Press ends over aligned with fly edges, keeping waistband square. Fold any cut selvedge edge under at an angle to hide it.

### Top Stitching
- Verify selvedge extends **1/4"–3/8" below the waistband seam** (to be caught in stitching).
- First pass: top stitch from the left fly front edge, down to and across the waist seam at **1/8" seam allowance**, catching selvedge underneath. Continue to the right fly, then halfway up the right fly.
- Second pass: starting again at the left fly, top stitch the remaining fly end, then across the **top edge** of the waistband.

---

## 5. 1873 Watch Pocket

*Installed after the waistband. Extends halfway up the waistband.*

- Mark **1/2" seam allowances** on sides/bottom and **7/8"** on the top of the lining. Trim. Mark **3/8"** on sides/bottom and **7/8"** on the pocket top.
- Press and fold the top seam, tuck raw edge under. Place lining in position.
- Top stitch a single row about **3/8" from the top**.
- Press remaining seam allowances to the wrong side.
- Place on the right front pocket: top extends ~midway up the waistband, bottom just below the front pocket opening.
- Top stitch sides and bottom about **1/8" from the edge**. Second row about **1/4" from the first**, parallel throughout.

---

## 6. Belt Loops II — Modern (Finishing)

- Align each belt loop square to the waistband, right sides together with the leg.
- Measure down **3/8"–1/2"** and bar tack across.
- Fold and press the loop up across the waistband, keeping it square.
- Bar tack across the bottom, about **1/8" from the bottom**.
- Fold the top edge so the fold is about **1/8" above the top of the waistband** (creates ease for thicker belts). Trim excess (leave at least 3/8" on the underside).
- Slide the loop down so the fold is even with the waistband top. Bar tack thickly across the top.

---

## 7. Waistband Buttonhole

- Draw a horizontal line across the center of the waistband on the left side (**3/4" from top and bottom**).
- Mark the buttonhole width: about **1/2" from the end** of the waistband, then another **3/4"** for the width (~1/8" wider than your buttons).
- Cut open and work the buttonhole stitch as per the earlier fly buttonhole instructions.

---

## 8. Hemming

- Mark points **7/8" from the bottom** around the leg (gives two 3/8" allowances plus 1/8" for fabric width when turning).
- Fold hem toward wrong side, aligning fold with the marks. Press with fingers, then iron (focus on the seams).
- Fold the raw edge under itself, forming the final hem.
- Sew from the wrong side using a **3/8" seam**, turning a little at a time as you go. Use a **seam jumper** at thick seam crossings (especially the flat-felled inseam).
- On the outside, turn up the hem about **1 1/2"**. Press crease with fingers, then iron.
- Consider waiting to finalize the hem until after all construction is complete.

---

## 9. The Back Cinch Belt

*1873 jeans feature.*

### Preparation
- Trace the pattern onto selvedge denim, mirroring for the other side. Cut out both pieces.
- Mark finished seam lines on each piece using the pattern and a ruler.
- Press seam allowances on both ends. Press the raw non-selvedge edge, then fold and press the selvedge over everything. Tidy up ends.
- Draw a center line across the length.
- Verify both pieces are identical in size and that the buckle fits.

### Sewing
- Sew across the length from end to end, aligning stitches with the selvedge underneath.
- On the left cinch belt (selvedge pointing down from right side), slide on the buckle facing correctly and overlap the back by ~1". Secure with top stitching through all layers (use box stay for 1873 style).
- Secure the right cinch belt in the buckle with ~1" extending past.

### Attaching to the Jeans
- Center the assembly vertically on the waistband seam and horizontally on the back. Measure from center back to each end to verify symmetry.
- Mark end positions in chalk. Make vertical lines **1" from each end** on the cinch belt.
- Sew **box stays** at each end, about **1/8" from the belt edges** and across the marked lines.

---

## 10. Leather Washers (Optional)

- Use lightweight leather (2–4 oz), an awl, a **1/8" hole punch**, and a **1/2" leather punch**.
- Punch out washers with the 1/2" punch.
- Find center with an awl, then use the 1/8" punch to make the center hole using the awl hole as a guide.
- Make enough for all rivets and buttons.

---

## 11. Rivets

### Rivet Positions
- Top edge of front pocket opening (~1/4" from waistband)
- Lower front pocket opening
- Top of watch pocket (may not apply to 1873)
- Tops of back pockets
- Optionally, bottom of fly (1873 version)

### Installation
- Use an awl to poke through from the right side at each position. Open the hole from the back as well.
- Insert rivet backing into the leather washer (optional), then into the hole from the wrong side.
- Place assembly on a small metal anvil (a block of wood underneath helps protect the table).
- Place the rivet on top, position the setting tool, and give a few light taps to start, then firmly hammer to secure.

---

## 12. Buttons

### Marking Button Positions
- Align the fly naturally, pull back the top layer to reveal buttonholes. Mark a point in each eyelet with a white pencil.
- **Suspender buttons (waistband):** First buttons aligned with the top edge of the front pocket. Second ~2 1/2" from the first (depends on your suspenders). Mark ~1/2" below the waistband top. Repeat for the other leg.
- **Back:** Two points ~2" from center back, 1/2" below the waistband top.

### Sewn-On Buttons
- Double up thread on the needle, knot the cut ends.
- Stitch from the underside just to the side of the button position. Thread the button (across opposite holes).
- Keep threads loose — button should **float ~1/4" above** the waistband.
- Take 4 stitches through each set of holes (8 total; doubled thread = 16 layers). Rotate stitches slightly like clock hands. Fine-tune the button height after the second stitch.
- Pass thread to the top side (not through the button). **Wrap the thread shank** 4–5 times, pulling each wrap tight.
- Push needle through the thread mass 3 times in alternating directions to lock. Trim close.

### Doughnut Buttons
- Place optional leather washer (smooth side down) onto the pronged backing.
- Poke prongs through the fly/waistband at the desired location (diagonal orientation to distribute stress).
- Place the button on top. Give a couple light taps with the setting tool to loosely set prongs.
- Verify prongs are bending correctly, then hammer firmly to finish.
