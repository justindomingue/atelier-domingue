# Drafting the Back Cinch Belt

Source: https://masterclasses.historical-tailoring.com/lesson/drafting-the-back-cinch-belt/

The back cinch belt is used on the 1873 model and other selvedge denim jeans.

## Steps

### 1. Draw the baseline

Draw a horizontal line **5"** long (point 0 to point 5). For larger sizes, the
length may be increased or graduated rulers used.

### 2. Mark the vertical width lines

At each end, mark lines square (perpendicular) to the baseline:

| End | Distance each side | Total finished width |
|-----|-------------------|---------------------|
| Point 0 (wide end) | 5/8" | 1 1/4" |
| Point 5 (narrow end) | 1/2" | 1" |

The 1/2" (1" total) at the narrow end is standard and fits most buckles.

### 3. Draw the outlines

Connect the marks top-to-top and bottom-to-bottom. This gives the **finished
size** — a tapered shape, wider at one end.

### 4. Add seam allowances (long edges)

Extend the vertical marks further:

| End | Additional each side |
|-----|---------------------|
| Point 0 (wide end) | 3/4" |
| Point 5 (narrow end) | 5/8" |

Draw the top and bottom SA lines, extending them slightly past the ends.

### 5. Add seam allowances (short ends)

Add **1/2"** on both ends (shown in red on the draft).

## Draft visualization

The final draft shows five horizontal lines (SA top, finished top, center,
finished bottom, SA bottom), vertical construction lines at both ends spanning
the full SA height, and the 1/2" end SA verticals highlighted in red.

## Cutting note

Cut one long edge on the **selvedge** of the fabric. The selvedge falls just
below the horizontal center line (the bottom long edge, `f_bl` to `f_br`).

---

## 1873 vs Modern variant

The back cinch belt is **1873-only**. It is not included in the modern variant.
