# The Front Pockets — Construction Guide

---

## 1. Preparing the Pocket Bags

- Mark **3/8" seam allowances** on the bottom and inner edges of the pocket facings. (The curved waist and side seams do not need marking.)
- Press the bottom and inner seams to the wrong side. At the overlapping corner, manipulate the fabric so there's no excess poking out.
- Open the pocket bags (cut on the fold) and place the facings into position on opposite sides.
- Top stitch through all layers along the inner and bottom edges of the pocket facings, keeping about **1/16"–1/8" from the edge**.

---

## 2. Modern Watch Pocket

*For modern jeans only. For 1873 jeans, the watch pocket is installed after the waistband.*

### Options for the Top Edge
- No selvedge visible (standard)
- Selvedge folded under (thinner top edge)
- Seam allowance removed and replaced with a selvedge strip (shows off selvedge subtly)

### Preparing the Watch Pocket
- Mark **1/2" seam allowances** on sides and bottom, and **7/8"** on the top edge of the watch pocket lining. Trim the lining.
- Mark **3/8"** on sides and bottom of the watch pocket, and **7/8"** along the top.
- Press the top seam allowance to the wrong side, tuck raw edge under, and press firmly.
- Place the lining in position.
- Top stitch the top: first row about **1/16" from the top edge**, second row **3/8" from the top**. Match the yoke seam spacing.
- Press remaining seam allowances to the wrong side.

### Positioning on the Pocket Facing
- Place on the **right pocket facing**, about **1"–1 1/2" below the top edge** and about **1 1/2" from the side seam**, following the angle of the waist seam.
- Top stitch around outer edges about **1/16"–1/8" from the edge**.
- Second row of top stitching about **3/8"** from the first, tapering to about **1/2"** at the top.

---

## 3. Installing the Pockets — Part I

*Applies to both 1873 and modern jeans.*

### Cutting and Aligning
- Lay the pocket bag right side (inside) up. Place the front leg right side down with the pocket opening on the side without the facing.
- Align the pocket bag and leg edges. Use the small pattern piece cut from the front leg pattern to help align.
- Trace the pocket opening onto the pocket bag and cut it out.

### Sewing the Pocket Opening
- Align the pocket bag and leg openings. Stitch along the entire pocket opening using a **3/8" seam allowance**.
- With steam, gently **stretch the pocket opening** in the seam allowance at the curved area until it forms a nearly straight line.
- Turn to the right side and press the pocket back up and away from the leg, manipulating around the curve.
- Turn the pocket bag to the wrong side of the leg. Press the edge of the pocket opening, setting back the pocket bag about **1/8"** so it can't be seen from the right side.

### Top Stitching
- Top stitch about **1/8" from the edge** (single row for 1873 jeans).
- **Modern jeans:** Add a second row about **3/8" from the edge**, maintaining the yoke spacing.

---

## 4. Installing the Pockets — Part II

### Sewing the Pocket Bag
- Lay the pocket bag facing-side down, moving the leg out of the way.
- Fold the pocket in half (inside out) with the facing on the outside. Align the bottom and remaining edge.
- Sew along the bottom edge (taper around any curves into the folded edge), then up along the curved seam to the side seam.
- At corners, take a single **diagonal stitch at 45 degrees** rather than a sharp corner — this makes turning smoother.
- **Clip the corners**, then turn right side out. Use a point pressing tool for crisp corners.
- Press the pocket bag well.

### Top Stitching and Closing
- Top stitch along the curved and bottom edges, about **1/8"–1/4" from the edge**.
- Sew across the top of the pocket along the waist seam, about **1/4" from the edge** through all layers. Fold the top of the pocket into final position before sewing. End stitching at the end of the pocket underneath.

### Side Seam Attachment
- Starting at the waist seam, sew down along the side seam through all layers.
- Just above the pocket, pause and **slide the front leg at the side seam forward about 1/8"** away from the facing underneath. This gives the pocket room to curve around the body and makes it easier to get your hand in.
- Continue sewing to the bottom of the pocket underneath.
- Repeat for the other pocket.
