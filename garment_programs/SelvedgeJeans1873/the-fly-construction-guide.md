# The Fly — Construction Guide

Three fly methods are covered: the 1873 two-piece button fly, the one-piece fly, and the zipper fly (instructions forthcoming from the course).

---

## 1. Preparing the 1873 Button Fly

### Marking the Fly
- Overcast the long edges and curves to the bottom point. (If using a selvedge along one edge, this saves work.)
- Press the fly in half, **wrong sides together**, keeping the top edge set back from the selvedge by about **1/16"**.
- Baste together along the open edge.
- Mark the inlay area at the top edge clearly. Add two marks **3/8" to left and right** of the inlay mark — the left mark is the seam line (waistband position), the right mark is 3/4" from that (waistband buttonhole position).

### Buttonhole Positions
- Mark the lower buttonhole about **2"–2 1/2" from the bottom edge** (leave room for top stitching below it and around the curve).
- Space additional buttonholes evenly between the lower and top positions, generally **1 1/2"–2 1/2" apart**.
- Square the marks across the buttonholes.
- Mark the outer edge about **3/8" from the folded edge**.
- Mark the inner edge about **1/8"–1/4" wider** than your buttons.

---

## 2. Preparing the One-Piece Fly

- Use a piece of selvedge denim about **double the fly length plus 2"**. Width: about **4" total** (1 3/4" finished width front and back plus 1/2" seam allowance).
- Fold and press the **1/2" seam allowance** to the wrong side.
- Fold the fly in half lengthwise, positioning the folded edge **just behind the selvedge (~1/16")** so it won't show in the finished fly. Press.
- Sew the fly closed along its length, stitching just beside the selvedge.
- Fold and press the fly across the middle.

### Buttonhole Positions
- Orient the fly as it would appear in the finished jeans. Place the buttonhole section on the jeans front, aligning the fold with the fly extension.
- Mark the waist position and add marks **3/8" above** (top waistband buttonhole) and **3/8" below** (seam line).
- Mark the lower buttonhole about **2"–2 1/4" above the fold**.
- Space remaining buttonholes evenly between lower and upper marks.
- Square marks across, mark outer edge **3/8" from the folded edge**, and inner edge at your desired buttonhole width (**~1/8" larger than buttons**; 3/4" buttonholes are typical).

---

## 3. Preparing the Buttonholes

### Cutting
- Use a leather punch (3/16"–1/4" setting) at the intersection of layout marks near the folded edge.
- Cut the straight part with a **1/2" chisel**, sliding along rather than lifting to maintain alignment.
- Cut small triangular pieces on both sides of the hole with scissors, forming a **teardrop shape**.

### Felling
- Secure thread with 3–4 stitches in place at the base.
- Fell densely around the buttonhole, keeping about **1/8" depth** and **1/16"–1/8" spacing** between stitches. Catch both layers.

### Gimp
- Attach a piece of gimp (~1/16" thick) to give structure and raise the buttonhole.
- Secure with a few stitches, leaving a loop to slide the gimp through.
- Use a **couching stitch** (diagonal stitch over the gimp) to attach the gimp around the buttonhole, keeping it about **1/16" from the edge**.
- Secure both ends with couching stitches in place, leaving **~1" tails**.

---

## 4. Working the Buttonholes

### The Buttonhole Stitch
- Use about 1 yard of **waxed silk buttonhole twist**, knotted at one end.
- Pass the needle between the two layers at the end of the buttonhole, then through the center of the buttonhole through all layers.
- Before pulling the needle through, wrap the two strands **clockwise underneath the needle point**.
- Pull gently to form a **purl**, then pull upward so the purl sits on the upper part of the buttonhole.
- Repeat along the straight part (~1/8" depth, closely spaced).

### Around the Eyelet
- Gradually move the purl to the **top of the gimp** (like wheel spokes) to maintain density.
- Taper purls back to the side position as you exit the eyelet.

### Finishing — Bartack
- At the end, make one last stitch without forming the purl.
- Pull gimp ends firmly through to the underside.
- Make **4 bartack stitches** across the end of the buttonhole, wrapping around the gimp underneath.
- Make about **8 small loop stitches** sideways around the bartack.
- Pass needle to the underside, trim excess gimp past the bartack.
- Pass needle through the underside of all buttonhole stitches (3 passes, alternating direction). Trim thread.

## 5. 1873 Button Fly — Part I (Right Leg)

### Preparing the Right Leg
- Mark a line **3/8" from the edge** of the fly extension on the right leg. Also mark **3/8" from the bottom** of the fly extension.
- **Trim off the fly extension**, then **clip 3/8"** to the mark at the bottom.
- Overcast the raw edges along the entire left fly from waist to crotch. Stop and restart thread at the clip.

### Button Extension
- Cut a rectangle **4 1/4" wide** by about 3" longer than the fly extension. (Width = 1 3/4" × 2 + two 3/8" seam allowances.)
- Overcast the long edges.
- Fold in half lengthwise, right sides together. About **1/2" from the bottom**, draw a line at ~30 degrees sloping toward the fold. Sew along this line.
- Trim seam allowance to **1/4"**, tapering to **1/8"** at the fold. Turn right side out, press firmly.
- Align the button extension on the right leg (three raw edges aligned). Mark about **1" from the bottom** and line up with the clipped seam.
- Sew to the right leg using a **3/8" seam allowance** from waist to the bottom clip.
- Open and press all seams towards the pocket.
- Top stitch on the leg side of the seam through all layers. At the bottom clip, fold the curved section out of the way and continue stitching ~1/2" further for hidden strength.

---

## 6. 1873 Button Fly — Part II (Left Leg)

### Fly Facing
- Cut a fly facing from scrap denim using the fly piece as a template, adding **3/8" seam allowance** along the straight edge and **1" inlay** at the top.
- Cut off the **3/8" fly extension** on the left leg and **clip 3/8"** at the bottom.
- Lay the fly facing on the left leg, aligning the bottom edge with the clip. Stitch from waist to clip using a **3/8" seam allowance**.
- Press the fly facing and seam allowances away from the leg, then press under. Set back the edge about **1/8"**.
- Top stitch from waist to clip, about **1/8" from the edge**. Trim the fly facing flush with the waist.

### Attaching the Button Fly
- On the wrong side of the left leg, align the button fly on the facing, set back **~1/8"** from the edge, bottom edge overlapping the clip by **~1/4"**.
- On the right side, trace around the outside curved edge of the fly pattern.
- Top stitch along the chalked line, curving into the clip. Add a second parallel row about **1/8" inside** the first.

### Joining the Front Legs
- Lay both legs **right sides together**, aligning crotch points and clips.
- Sew from the clip through both legs and the button catch underneath to the crotch point using a **3/8" seam allowance**.
- Open to the right side. Mark a line across the fly about **1" from the bottom**, and a point on that line about **1/2" from the fly edge**.
- Top stitch from the crotch point along the folded edge, up to the horizontal line, over 1/2", then taper back down about **3/8"** from the original stitch line. Ensure all seam allowances point toward the left leg.

## 7. One-Piece Fly — Part I (Left Leg)

### Preparing the Left Leg
- Lay the left leg **wrong side up**. Fold the fly extension **3/8" toward the wrong side** and press.
- Turn the folded edge and entire fly seam over a further **3/8"**. Gently stretch the seam allowance in the curved area.
- Press from waist down to crotch with steam.
- Mark the bottom of the fly extension on the right side (where it goes from 3 to 2 layers).
- Top stitch from the right side using a **3/8" seam allowance**, curving in toward the mark at the fabric edge.

### Attaching the Fly
- Lay the left leg right side down, place the buttonhole fly on top (buttonholes facing up).
- Align the waist mark with the waistband, set the front fly edge back **~1/8"** from the leg edge.
- Measure the distance from the front fly seam edge to the selvedge edge. On the right side, draw a parallel line that same distance from the front edge.
- Draw a curve at the bottom toward the previous stitching.
- Sew top stitching along the marked line, ending where the previous stitching ends. Check alignment every inch or so.
- Add a second row of top stitching just inside the first, tapering into the first row around the curve.

---

## 8. One-Piece Fly — Part II (Right Leg & Crotch Seam)

### Right Leg
- Mark **3/8" from the fly extension edge** on the right leg, then **cut off the fly extension**.
- Lay the left leg **wrong side up**, fold the fly into position. Lay the right leg on top **right side up** (wrong sides together).
- Set back the right leg's fly seam about **1/8"** from the fly edge. Mark the fly at the waist seam for alignment reference.
- Holding everything, move the left leg out from underneath (opening the fly flat). Take the right leg + fly to the machine.
- Stitch from waist toward the bottom of the fly with a **3/8" seam allowance**, stopping just before the left leg crotch seam fold.
- Open the fly on the right leg, press well.
- Top stitch **1/16"–1/8" from the edge** of the fly, ending at the same stopping point.

### Crotch Seam
- The right crotch seam should be folded to about **1/4" width** from the fly construction. Maintain that width around the curve.
- Gently stretch the right crotch curve in the seam allowance only. Crease and press over ~1/4".
- Lay the left crotch seam on top of the right, with each folded edge covering the opposing raw edge as they overlap.
- Top stitch from the bottom of the crotch curve through all layers, **~1/8" from the folded edge** of the left leg, stopping just before the fly.
- Add a second row about **3/8" (or just under)** from the first, catching the other seam edge.
