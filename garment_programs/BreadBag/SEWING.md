# Kinto-Style Bread Bag — Sewing Instructions

A lined, roll-top bread bag inspired by the Kinto Futo Bread Bag. Uses a "bag-in-a-bag" construction where the lining is shorter (no roll-top) and the whole thing gets turned right-side-out through a gap.

## Pieces to Cut

All pieces include 1 cm seam allowance.

| Piece | Fabric | Qty | Cut Size (SMALL) | Cut Size (LARGE) |
|-------|--------|-----|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Outer Body** | Main (linen) | 2 | 20 × 36.5 cm (notched) | 26 × 44.5 cm (notched) |
| **Lining Body** | Cotton canvas | 2 | 20 × 28.5 cm (notched) | 26 × 34.5 cm (notched) |
| **Strap** | Main (linen) | 2 | 3 × 61 cm | 4 × 75 cm |

### How the dimensions are derived

- **Body width** = `width + depth` (e.g. 11 + 9 = 20 cm)
- **Outer body height** = `height + roll_top + depth/2` (e.g. 24 + 8 + 4.5 = 36.5 cm)
- **Lining body height** = `height + depth/2` (e.g. 24 + 4.5 = 28.5 cm) — no roll-top
- **Bottom corner notch** = `depth/2 × depth/2` (e.g. 4.5 × 4.5 cm) — cut from each bottom corner
- **Strap width** = `strap_width × 2` (e.g. 1.5 × 2 = 3 cm)
- **Strap length** = `width + 2 × strap_tail` (e.g. 11 + 2 × 25 = 61 cm)

## Sewing Order

### 1. Make the Straps

1. Fold each strap in half lengthwise, wrong sides together (finished width: 1.5 cm small / 2 cm large)
2. Press
3. Tuck the short raw ends in and topstitch along the open long edge, ~3 mm from the fold, closing the ends as you go

### 2. Assemble the Outer Bag

1. Place the two **outer body** panels right sides together
2. Sew each **side seam** from the top down to the notch at 1 cm SA
3. Sew the **bottom seam** (the narrow edge between the two notches) at 1 cm SA
4. **Box the corners**: open each notch flat so the cut edges align. Stitch across at 1 cm SA. This forms the flat bottom.

### 3. Assemble the Lining

1. Sew side seams, bottom seam, and box the corners — same as the outer
2. **Leave a ~10 cm turning gap** in the center of the bottom seam

### 4. Join Outer + Lining

1. Turn the outer bag **right side out**
2. Slip the outer bag *inside* the lining (right sides together — the lining is still inside-out)
3. Align the top edges and pin all around
4. Stitch around the **top edge** at 1 cm SA
5. **Turn right-side-out** through the gap in the lining bottom
6. Press the top edge
7. **Topstitch** ~3 mm from the top edge to keep it crisp
8. Slipstitch or machine-stitch the lining turning gap closed

### 5. Attach Straps

1. Centre each strap across one face of the bag, aligned with the top edge (or just below the roll-top fold line)
2. Topstitch across the strap at the centre to secure it — the tails should extend equally from each side

### 6. Roll-Top Closure

The extra 8 cm (SMALL) or 10 cm (LARGE) of outer body above the lining is the **roll-top**. Fold it down twice (each fold ~4 cm) to close the bag. Cross the straps from each face over the roll and tie in a knot on top.
